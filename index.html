Projects

Robotic Manipulation

Before taking a look at the final product in the video, I will give you some context to understand the reasoning behind the task breakdown structure.

Task 1:
Simulate the robotic arm moving in 3D space, using the developed inverse kinematics model.
Task 2:
Translate the inverse kinematics model onto a real robot and demonstrate the position control capabilities in the 3D plane.
Task 3:
Demonstrate speed control capabilities in the 3D plane.
Task 4:
Modelling a real world application. We incorporated current control enabling control over motor torque in order to model a robot that would clean the seashore and ocean when submerged under media of varying density.

This was one of the coolest and most rewarding projects I've undertaken!

Mars Rover

Take a look at the cool Mars Rover we made!

In the video, I speak in depth about the various subsystems; however, to overview, the aim of this project was to design, program, build and test a semi-autonomous, self-charging, robust rover that is capable of scanning its surroundings and accurately traversing to objects of interest, whilst avoiding obstacles.

P.S. In the demo, we are depicting the obstacle avoidance capabilities of the rover. That's why the most efficient route isn't taken.
